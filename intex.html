Class 12 NCERT Part 1 - Electricity & Magnetism

Chapter 1: Electric Charges & Fields

Key Formulas

F = k (q₁q₂)/r²
F = Force, k = Coulomb's constant (9×10⁹ Nm²C⁻²), q₁,q₂ = charges, r = distance
E = F/q = kQ/r²
E = Electric field, F = Force, q = test charge, Q = source charge
Φ = ∮E·dA = Q/ε₀
Φ = Electric flux, E = Electric field, Q = enclosed charge, ε₀ = permittivity of free space
E = σ/(2ε₀) (Infinite plane sheet)
σ = Surface charge density

Important Board Questions

1. State Coulomb’s law and express it in vector form. Derive the expression for electric field due to a point charge.
2. Derive an expression for electric field intensity at a point on the axial and equatorial line of an electric dipole.
3. State Gauss’s theorem and apply it to find electric field due to an infinitely long straight charged wire.
4. What is electric flux? Write its SI unit. Using Gauss’s law, find electric field due to a uniformly charged spherical shell.

Key Results

• **Coulomb’s Law**: Force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance.

• **Electric Dipole Moment (p)**: p = q × 2a (where 2a = distance between charges).

• **Gauss’s Law**: Total flux through a closed surface is (1/ε₀) times the charge enclosed.

• **Electric Field due to Infinite Plane Sheet**: Uniform and independent of distance.

Chapter 2: Electrostatic Potential & Capacitance

Key Formulas

V = W/q = kQ/r
V = Potential, W = Work done, q = test charge, Q = source charge
C = Q/V
C = Capacitance, Q = Charge, V = Potential difference
U = ½ CV² = ½ QV = ½ Q²/C
U = Energy stored in a capacitor
C = ε₀A/d (Parallel Plate Capacitor)
A = Area of plates, d = separation between plates

Important Board Questions

1. Derive an expression for electric potential at a point due to an electric dipole.
2. Explain the principle of a capacitor and derive the expression for capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor.
3. What is the effect of dielectric on capacitance? Derive the expression for capacitance with dielectric.
4. Derive the expression for energy stored in a capacitor and energy density of a capacitor.

Key Results

• **Potential due to Dipole**: V = (kp cosθ)/r² (axial) and V = 0 (equatorial).

• **Capacitance with Dielectric**: Increases by a factor of K (dielectric constant).

• **Energy Stored in Capacitor**: U = ½ CV².

• **Capacitors in Series**: 1/Ceq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...

• **Capacitors in Parallel**: Ceq = C₁ + C₂ + ...

Chapter 3: Current Electricity

Key Formulas

I = nAevd
I = Current, n = charge density, A = area, e = electron charge, vd = drift velocity
V = IR (Ohm’s Law)
V = Potential difference, I = Current, R = Resistance
R = ρL/A
ρ = Resistivity, L = Length, A = Cross-sectional area
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
P = Power dissipated

Important Board Questions

1. Derive the expression for drift velocity of electrons in a conductor and hence deduce Ohm’s law.
2. Explain the variation of resistivity with temperature for conductors and semiconductors.
3. Derive the condition for balanced Wheatstone bridge using Kirchhoff’s laws.
4. What is potentiometer? Explain how it can be used to compare EMFs of two cells.

Key Results

• **Drift Velocity (vd)**: vd = (eEτ)/m where τ = relaxation time.

• **Resistivity (ρ)**: Increases with temperature for metals, decreases for semiconductors.

• **Wheatstone Bridge**: Balanced when P/Q = R/S.

• **Potentiometer**: More accurate than voltmeter as it draws no current.

Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism

Key Formulas

F = q(v × B)
F = Force, q = charge, v = velocity, B = magnetic field
F = I(l × B)
F = Force, I = current, l = length of conductor, B = magnetic field
B = (μ₀I)/(2πr)
B = magnetic field, μ₀ = permeability of free space, I = current, r = distance
f = qB/(2πm)
f = cyclotron frequency, q = charge, B = magnetic field, m = mass

Important Board Questions

1. Derive the expression for force on a current carrying conductor in a magnetic field.
2. Explain the principle, construction and working of a cyclotron with a neat diagram.
3. State Biot-Savart law and apply it to find magnetic field at a point on the axis of a circular current loop.
4. Two long parallel wires carrying currents I₁ and I₂ in the same direction are separated by distance d. Derive the expression for force per unit length between them.

Key Results

• The magnetic force on a charged particle is always perpendicular to its velocity.

• The path of a charged particle in a uniform magnetic field is circular if v⊥B.

• The radius of the circular path is r = mv/qB.

• Cyclotron frequency is independent of the particle's velocity.

Chapter 5: Magnetism and Matter

Key Formulas

τ = m × B
τ = torque, m = magnetic moment, B = magnetic field
U = -m·B
U = potential energy, m = magnetic moment, B = magnetic field
B = μ₀(H + M)
B = magnetic field, H = magnetic intensity, M = magnetization
χ = M/H
χ = magnetic susceptibility, M = magnetization, H = magnetic intensity

Important Board Questions

1. Define magnetic dipole moment. Derive an expression for torque on a magnetic dipole in uniform magnetic field.
2. Explain the elements of Earth's magnetic field with the help of a diagram.
3. Distinguish between diamagnetic, paramagnetic and ferromagnetic materials with examples.
4. What is hysteresis? Explain hysteresis loop for a ferromagnetic material.

Key Results

• Magnetic dipole moment of a current loop is m = IA.

• The Earth's magnetic field has three components: declination, inclination/dip, and horizontal component.

• Diamagnetic materials have negative susceptibility (χ < 0).

• Ferromagnetic materials show hysteresis - their magnetization depends on history.

Chapter 6: Electromagnetic Induction

Key Formulas

ε = -dΦ/dt
ε = induced emf, Φ = magnetic flux, t = time
Φ = B·A = BAcosθ
Φ = magnetic flux, B = magnetic field, A = area, θ = angle between B and A
ε = Blv
ε = motional emf, B = magnetic field, l = length, v = velocity
L = NΦ/I
L = self-inductance, N = number of turns, Φ = magnetic flux, I = current

Important Board Questions

1. State Faraday's laws of electromagnetic induction. Derive the expression for induced emf.
2. Explain Lenz's law and show that it is in accordance with the law of conservation of energy.
3. Derive an expression for motional emf induced in a conductor moving in uniform magnetic field.
4. What are eddy currents? Explain their applications and how they can be minimized.

Key Results

• Faraday's law: The induced emf is equal to the negative rate of change of magnetic flux.

• Lenz's law: The direction of induced current opposes the change causing it.

• Self-inductance opposes the change in current through a coil.

• Eddy currents are circulating currents induced in bulk conductors.

Chapter 7: Alternating Current

Key Formulas

I = I₀sin(ωt)
I = instantaneous current, I₀ = peak current, ω = angular frequency, t = time
V = V₀sin(ωt)
V = instantaneous voltage, V₀ = peak voltage
I₀ = V₀/Z
Z = impedance = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²)
X_L = ωL, X_C = 1/(ωC)
X_L = inductive reactance, X_C = capacitive reactance

Important Board Questions

1. Derive an expression for impedance of series LCR circuit using phasor diagram.
2. What is resonant frequency in LCR circuit? Derive its expression.
3. Explain the principle, construction and working of a transformer.
4. Why is AC preferred over DC for power transmission? Explain with calculations.

Key Results

• RMS value of AC is I₀/√2 and V₀/√2.

• In purely inductive circuit, voltage leads current by π/2.

• In purely capacitive circuit, current leads voltage by π/2.

• At resonance (ω = 1/√(LC)), impedance is minimum and current is maximum.
